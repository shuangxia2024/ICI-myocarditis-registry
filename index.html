Background

About the Registry

Immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI) are monoclonal antibodies targeting surface receptors on T-lymphocytes — including CTLA-4, PD-1, LAG-3 — or their ligands such as PD-L1, designed to boost the immune response against cancer. They have become cornerstone treatments across melanoma, lung, renal, head and neck, urothelial, hepatocellular, and many other cancer types, with indications continuing to expand.

However, ICI-induced immune activation can lead to autoimmune-related adverse events (irAEs) affecting any organ. Among these, cardiomyotoxicities — encompassing inflammation of the heart and peripheral skeletal muscles — are uncommon but the most fatal irAEs, with mortality rates up to 50%. These myotoxicities are characterized by inflammatory infiltration with macrophages and cytotoxic auto-reactive T-cells targeting muscular antigens such as α-myosin, with the heart and peripheral muscles specifically and concomitantly affected.

Clinically, patients may experience life-threatening arrhythmias, cardiogenic shock, or severe myositis with invalidating dysphagia or respiratory muscle failure mimicking myasthenic syndrome — potentially requiring mechanical ventilation.

Why a Registry?

An in-depth understanding of ICI myotoxicity incidence, predictors, and prognostic impact is crucial for clinicians managing cancer patients. Because these events are very rare, meaningful insights require large-scale multinational collaboration — beyond what single-centre cohorts or clinical trials can provide.

Registry Leadership

This ICI-myocarditis registry is led by Prof. Douglas B. Johnson (Vanderbilt University Medical Center), with scientific direction from Prof. Joe-Elie Salem (Sorbonne University / Hôpital Pitié-Salpêtrière, Paris) and Prof. Javid Moslehi (University of California San Francisco).

Become a Contributing Center

We welcome institutions worldwide. Participation involves secure online data entry via REDCap (hosted at Vanderbilt University Medical Center). Each site retains access only to its own submitted cases via unique, password-protected access codes.

Authorship Criteria
1 case Contributing center acknowledgment
≥5 cases One co-author on registry publications
≥15 cases 1 coauthor + 1 investigator
≥20 cases 2 coauthors
Data Entry Secure REDCap platform; each site accesses only its own cases
Privacy Fully de-identified data; no patient names or record numbers collected
Ethics IRB #222075; NCT04294771. Local ethics approval required before submission
Data Rights Data not shared with third parties without explicit written approval from contributing sites
✉   Contact us to join

Storage & Access. All data are stored on HIPAA-compliant, encrypted REDCap servers hosted at Vanderbilt University Medical Center. Only the Principal Investigator and scientific directors have access to the complete dataset. All data access activities are logged and monitored.

Security. Data transmission uses SSL/TLS encryption. No identifiable patient information is collected. Multi-factor authentication is required for dataset access. Regular security audits are conducted.

Patient Privacy. All data are fully de-identified before registry entry. Dates are transformed to relative time intervals (e.g., days from ICI initiation to myocarditis onset).

Data Sharing. Data will not be shared with any third party outside the research team without explicit written approval from contributing sites. No commercial use of data is permitted without separate agreements.
